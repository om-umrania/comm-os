# Minto Pyramid Principle & The Rule of 3

## Concept
Created by Barbara Minto at McKinsey, this principle dictates that communication should be structured like a pyramid: start with the main idea (BLUF), and group supporting arguments logically underneath it. 

The **Rule of 3** states that people can only comfortably hold three items in their working memory. Any list of reasons or arguments should be ruthlessly grouped into exactly three categories.

## The Standard
After delivering the BLUF, support it with exactly three categorized pillars.
- Provide a label for the pillar.
- Provide 1 sentence of context per pillar.

## Example
**BLUF:** "We are upgrading to Pinecone to solve our latency issues."
**Rule of 3:**
1. **Speed:** It reduces query latency from 800ms to 50ms.
2. **Scale:** It handles our projected 10x data growth without re-indexing downtime.
3. **Cost:** The managed infrastructure is 20% cheaper than maintaining our own instances.
